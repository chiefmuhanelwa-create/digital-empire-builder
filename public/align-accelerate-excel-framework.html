The pattern in creation

Genesis 1:1 — In the beginning (TIME) God created the heaven (SPACE) and the earth (MATTER). Time is invisible, and it came first. In the same way, who you BE — your alignment — is invisible, and it must come before what you DO (Space) and what you HAVE (Matter). God began with the unseen. So must you.

The one rule

The sequence is not optional. You cannot accelerate a car that is facing the wrong wall. First you Align. Then you Accelerate. Then you Excel. Skip a step and you build speed in the wrong direction.

How to use this guide

Read the phase you scored lowest on first. Do the "Your move this week" at the bottom of that page. Don't rush ahead — master one phase, then climb to the next.

Phase 1 of 3 — The Identity Phase
1Align · Identity · BE · Time

Align

"Be fruitful…" (Genesis 1:28) · "Meditate therein day and night…" (Joshua 1:8)

Time — the invisible, created first.

The principle

Internal alignment must come before external fruit. Alignment happens in the dark — in private, before anyone claps. You settle who you are, what you carry, and the habits that match the future you say you want.

What it looks like

  • You can say in one sentence what you want to be known for.
  • You have a daily habit you do before you touch your phone.
  • You see yourself as a steward and an owner — not just an employee.
  • Your faith is your operating system, not a slogan.

Example — Mr NoChill

In 2013 I was sleeping on the bathroom floor of the University of Pretoria with R47 in my account. In 2014 I bought a R6,000 phone with night-shift money and started creating content every single day — for free, for nobody. No office. No audience. I was stabilising my roots in private. Like Mary said before the miracle: "Let it be unto me according to your word." Agreement comes before the manifestation.

Your move this week

Write one sentence: what do you want to be known for? Then set one 30-minute habit you do before the world wakes up — prayer, study, or creating. Alignment starts there.

Phase 2 of 3 — The Momentum Phase
2Accelerate · Momentum · DO · Space

Accelerate

"Multiply & subdue…" (Genesis 1:28) · "Make your way prosperous…" (Joshua 1:8)

Space — the arena where you move and multiply.

The principle

When your alignment is right, God removes the friction. This is the phase of action — but the assignment is not to add, it is to multiply. You stop being busy and start building momentum. Acceleration is not for the talented; it is for the positioned.

What it looks like

  • You create and publish consistently — your work is in the world, not your head.
  • You build more than one income stream from your expertise.
  • You own a channel — an email or WhatsApp list no algorithm can take.
  • You treat every small opportunity as if it were worth R100,000.

Example — Mr NoChill

My first ever brand deal, in 2017, was R350 for one post. People laughed. But I had been faithful in the dark for years — so when the door opened, I was standing in front of it. In March 2019, one affiliate link earned R23,000 in a single day. In September 2025 I wrote a full book in 30 days — 30 minutes every morning at 05:00, before the noise. That is Joseph: prison to palace, because he stayed excellent in the prison. Position is what gets you paid.

Your move this week

Pick one thing in your head and put it in the world — a post, a checklist, a guide. Then start one owned list (email or WhatsApp). Multiply, don't just add.

Phase 3 of 3 — The Mastery Phase
3Excel · Mastery · HAVE · Matter

Excel

"Have dominion…" (Genesis 1:28) · "Have good success…" (Joshua 1:8)

Matter — the tangible dominion you leave behind.

The principle

To excel is not to be slightly better — it is to surpass. This is the realm of dominion, where you move from participant to ruler of your field. But dominion demands governance: ownership, accountability, and a hand back to those behind you.

What it looks like

  • Your work is so excellent your industry cannot ignore you — people come to you.
  • You are fully accountable — tax-compliant and managing your house, not just your talent.
  • You are building an asset your children or community can inherit.
  • You are lifting others as you rise.

Example — Mr NoChill

Daniel operated with an excellent spirit — ten times better than an entire empire (Daniel 6). That is the standard. But I learned the governance lesson the hard way: a SARS bill of just over R200,000, because I had managed my talent and not my house. True leaders don't run — I faced it and paid it. And when Instagram suspended my account of 780,000 followers overnight, my income survived, because I had built on ground I owned. Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu — a person is a person through others. Build what outlives you.

Your move this week

Put your house in order: get one financial or compliance task done. Then name one way you will lift someone coming behind you this month.
